Velocity-Dependent Potentials

Previously, we considered forces that are derivable from a potential function, that is \[\vec{F}_i^{(a)} = -\nabla_iV\] We can actually do better than this and generalize our potential function. What if the potential depends not only on the coordinates \(\{q_j\}\) but also the coordinate velocities \(\{\dot{q}_j\}\)? In this case, define \[Q_j = -\frac{\partial U}{\partial q_j}+\frac{d}{dt}\left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial \dot{q}_j} \right).\] D’Alembert’s principle says \[\frac{d}{dt}\left( \frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q}_j} \right)-\frac{\partial T}{\partial q_j}-Q_j = 0\] From this, we can see that our generalized potential still allows us to write \[\mathcal{L}= T-U\] so that d’Alembert’s principle again reduces to the Euler-Lagrange equations: \[\frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \dot{q}_j}-\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial q_j} = 0\]

Temperature

We have established that at thermal equilibrium, two systems than can exchange energy must satisfy \[\frac{\partial S_1}{\partial U_1} = \frac{\partial S_2}{\partial U_2}\] where \[S \equiv k_b\ln g(N,U).\] This directly leads to the idea of temperature. Our intuitive notion of temperature is the thing that’s the same when two objects are in thermal equilibrium. In other words, \[T_1 = T_2 \qquad \textrm{(at equilibrium)}\] We also know that if I dump energy into the system (ex: microwave a bowl a soup), then the temperature should increase. Putting this all together, we conclude that a reasonable definition of temperature is \[\frac{1}{T} = \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial U}\right)_N\]

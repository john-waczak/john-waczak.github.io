Sturm-Liouville Theory

From linear algebra we know that many function spaces can be identified as having a vector space structure (for example: the space \(C[a,b]\) of continuous functions on the interval \([a,b]\)). Furthermore, a particularly interesting example of a linear transformation on functions is the derivative for which \[\frac{d^n}{dx^n}\Big(\alpha f + g\Big) = \alpha\frac{d^n}{dx^n}f + \frac{d^n}{dx^n}g\]

From linear algebra we know that many function spaces can be identified as
having a vector space structure (for example: the space $C[a,b]$ of continuous
functions on the interval $[a,b]$). Furthermore, a particularly interesting
example of a linear transformation on functions is the derivative operation for which
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d^n}{dx^n}\Big(\alpha f + g\Big) = \alpha\frac{d^n}{dx^n}f + \frac{d^n}{dx^n}g
\end{equation}
where $\alpha$ is a scalar and $f,g$ are $n-$differentiable functions.
Differential equations and by extension the differential operators that encode
them arise all over the place.
